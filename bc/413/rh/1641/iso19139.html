Abstract:

The Satellite View with Shaded Relief of Hawaii map layer is a 100-meter resolution simulated natural-color image of Hawaii, with relief shading added to accentuate terrain features. Vegetation is generally green, with forests in darker green and grasslands or shrublands in lighter green. Areas of high reflectance, including urban areas, rock, and dry bare soil, are shown in shades of tan and pink. Very bright areas, such as snow and ice, are light blue. The image was produced by combining a mosaic of Landsat Thematic Mapper (TM) imagery from the Landsat 4 and Landsat 5 satellites with shaded relief generated from 100-meter elevation data extracted from the National Elevation Dataset (NED). This layer is part of the 1997-2014 edition National Atlas of the United States.

Purpose:

The 100-meter resolution satellite view with shaded relief data were developed to portray the ground cover of the United States at 1:1,000,000 scale. The image is intended for visual purposes only. The original Landsat Thematic Mapper imagery data and NED elevation data should be used for conducting analysis. No responsibility is assumed by the National Atlas of the United States in the use of these data.

Process Step:

The Satellite View with Shaded Relief image is a combination of the Satellite View and Grayscale Shaded Relief map layers that are distributed by the National Atlas of the United States. Characteristics and development of those two data sets are documented in their respective metadata. They were combined in the following steps: In Photoshop, a new image was created in CMYK color mode. Color separation settings were set to UCR, No black generation, 300 percent maximum CMY. The RGB National Atlas HI Satellite View 2013 image was opened and the Red band selected, copied, and pasted in its entirety into the Cyan band of the new image; the Green band was pasted into the Magenta band of the new image and the Blue band was copied into the Yellow band of the new image. National Atlas HI Grayscale Shaded Relief 2013 was copied and pasted into the Black band of the new image. Color separation settings were re-set to UCR, Maximum black generation, 400 percent maximum CMYK. The new CMYK image was then converted to RGB to create the final image. Ocean areas were set to an RGB value of (193,224,250). Non-U.S. areas were set to a RGB value of (240,235,211).
