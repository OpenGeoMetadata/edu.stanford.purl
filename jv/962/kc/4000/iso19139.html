Abstract:

This polygon shapefile portrays the state boundaries of the United States, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The map layer was created by extracting county polygon features from the CENSUS 2006 TIGER/Line files produced by the U.S. Census Bureau. These files were then merged into a single file and county boundaries within States were removed. This layer is part of the 1997-2014 edition of the National Atlas of the United States.

Purpose:

These data are intended for geographic display and analysis at the national level, and for large regional areas. The data should be displayed and analyzed at scales appropriate for 1:1,000,000-scale data. No responsibility is assumed by the National Atlas of the United States in the use of these data.

Evaluation Method Description:

Lineage:

Process Step:

The 1:1,000,000-scale county and State boundaries were derived from U.S. Census TIGER (Topologically Integrated Geographic Encoding and Reference system) data. The TIGER files were merged and generalized. County boundaries within States were removed using the ESRI dissolve tool.

Date And Time: 2007-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

All the 1:1,000,000-scale map layers were checked for vertical integration. Line positions were adjusted as necessary to remove overlaps. Wherever possible, linear features were separated by 300 meters, but were kept within 500 meters of their true location.

Date And Time: 2012-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The areas in the file were combined into large polygons, one for each State including associated islands, and for States that border the Great Lakes, one for the water area of each State. Population values were added from the 2010 Census information. The data were projected by region to the National Atlas Albers Equal Area Conic projection, fields were added for the perimeter measurement and square miles, the values were calculated using ArcMap, and the data were converted back to geographic coordinates. The Albers Equal Area Conic parameters used are available on request.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The map layer was saved in shapefile and in file geodatabase formats.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Data along the Canadian border were adjusted so that the Canadian and U.S. 1:1,000,000-scale data sets can be used together as seamlessly as possible. Problem areas were identified by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) and edits to the U.S. data were performed by the USGS. To identify problems, the National Atlas 1M States 2012 data set was projected to the NRCan working coordinate system, which is a Lambert Conformal Conic projection with standard parallels of 49 and 77 degrees, datum of NAD83, an XY tolerance of 0.001 meters, and an XY resolution of 0.0001 meters. The Canadian and U.S. data sets were displayed together and features were compared across the border. An initial set of comments and suggestions for edits were sent to the USGS by NRCan. NRCan used satellite imagery for reference during the editing process by converting the appropriate National Atlas 1:1,000,000-scale map layers and the 250K Canadian topographic data to KML and loading the KMLs into GoogleEarth. Using the cursor coordinates in the ArcMap status bar, NRCan visually navigated and correlated the ArcMap and GoogleEarth vectors. NRCan exported their findings and sent them to the USGS for correction and (or) further comment. USGS reviewed the Canadian findings and returned the data to NRCan with comments. The U.S. data were either edited, not edited and further explanation was requested, or not edited and changes were suggested for the Canadian data. This process was repeated until both NRCan and USGS felt that the data were satisfactorily harmonized.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00
