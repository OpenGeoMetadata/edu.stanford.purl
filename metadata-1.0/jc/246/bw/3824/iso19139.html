Abstract:

This line shapefile represents railroads in the conterminous United States and Alaska for 2012 at 1:1,000,000 scale. This layer is part of the 1997-2014 edition of the National Atlas of the United States.

Purpose:

These data are intended for geographic display and analysis at the national level, and for large regional areas. The data should be displayed and analyzed at scales appropriate for 1:1,000,000-scale data. No responsibility is assumed by the National Atlas of the United States in the use of these data.

Conceptual Consistency:

Measure Description:

The map layer was checked for topological consistency using ArcGIS. No other checks for logical consistency were performed on this map layer.

Completeness Omission:

Evaluation Method Description:

Lineage:

Process Step:

The Railway Network data were loaded and the attributes were edited to include a maximum of three railroad owner names and their corresponding reporting marks. Abandoned railroads and rail lines no longer in existence were identified and deleted. The attributes were reorganized, so that for tracks with multiple owners, the largest railroad is listed as Rrowner1, the next smaller railroad is listed as Rrowner2, and the smallest of the three railroads, if any, is listed as Rrowner3. Some tracks were adjusted inland from open water using Atlas_States as a guide. Tracks were generalized, particularly in congested areas, and merged where they run closely parallel to one another. The file was saved as a Shapefile.

Date And Time: 2010-08-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

All the 1:1,000,000-scale map layers were checked for vertical integration. Line positions were adjusted as necessary to remove overlaps. Wherever possible, linear features were separated by 300 meters, but were kept within 500 meters of their true location.

Date And Time: 2012-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The data were converted to an Albers Equal Area projection. Attribute fields for miles and kilometers were added, and the values were calculated using ArcGIS. The data were then converted back to geographic coordinates.

Date And Time: 2012-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The map layer was saved in shapefile and in file geodatabase formats.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Where railroad segments less than 0.1 KM in length were adjacent to longer segments with the same attributes, the segments were joined. All remaining railroads less than 0.1 KM in length were evaluated and were either joined to neighboring segments, or their length was adjusted to greater than 0.1 KM. The data were converted to an Albers Equal Area projection, values for miles and kilometers were calculated using ArcGIS, and the data were converted back to geographic coordinates.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Data along the Canadian border were adjusted so that the Canadian and U.S. 1:1,000,000-scale data sets can be used together as seamlessly as possible. Problem areas were identified by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) and edits to the U.S. data were performed by the USGS. To identify problems, the National Atlas 1M railroads 2012 data set was projected to the NRCan working coordinate system, which is a Lambert Conformal Conic projection with standard parallels of 49 and 77 degrees, datum of NAD83, an XY tolerance of 0.001 meters, and an XY resolution of 0.0001 meters. The Canadian and U.S. data sets were displayed together and features were compared across the border. An initial set of comments and suggestions for edits were sent to the USGS by NRCan. NRCan used satellite imagery for reference during the editing process by converting the appropriate National Atlas 1:1,000,000-scale map layers and the 250K Canadian topographic data to KML and loading the KMLs into GoogleEarth. Using the cursor coordinates in the ArcMap status bar, NRCan visually navigated and correlated the ArcMap and GoogleEarth vectors. NRCan exported their findings and sent them to the USGS for correction and (or) further comment. USGS reviewed the Canadian findings and returned the data to NRCan with comments. The U.S. data were either edited, not edited and further explanation was requested, or not edited and changes were suggested to for the Canadian data. This process was repeated until both NRCan and USGS felt that the data were satisfactorily harmonized.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Railroads along the Mexican border were aligned based on reference to ESRI Basemaps, Google Maps, and Bing Maps.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00
