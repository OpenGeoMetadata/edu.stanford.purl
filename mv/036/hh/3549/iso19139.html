Abstract:

This polygon shapefile depicts hazardous areas of relative soil erosion for Monterey County, California. Erosion can be defined as the wearing away of the land surface by flowing water, waves, or wind, or by such process as mass wasting and corrosion. Water and wind erosion are important in assessing the health of the soil and in assessing the soil's potential for different uses. Removal of increasing amounts of soil increasingly alters various properties and capabilities of the soil. Properties and qualities affected include bulk density, permeability, organic matter content, tilth, and can degrade surface water quality. In Monterey County, farming is a major source of employment and revenue; hence, protecting the county's soil resources is important. This layer is part of a collection describing Geology resources and constraints Monterey County, California.

Purpose:

The purpose of this map is to provide a comparison of relative soil erosion hazards for regional planning studies.

Process Step:

Soils are rated as to their erosion hazard potential in the Soil Survey of Monterey County, California (Cook, 1978). Cook classified the erosion potential into several classes, ranging from very high to minimal based on detailed field mapping and laboratory testing. For this study, these groups were combined into high, moderate, and low categories. The Soil Survey Geographic database for Monterey County database (NRCS, 1999) includes georeferenced digital soil mapping units. A table of the soil units in Monterey County and their subjective soil erosion was manually created and saved as a tab-delimited text file. This text file was joined to the SSURGO DBF file using ArcView 3.2a. The resultant polygons were dissolved into high, moderate, and low classes.
