Abstract:

This line shapefile includes Global Map data showing the major roads in the United States, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The data are a modified version of the National Atlas of the United States 1:1,000,000-Scale Major Roads of the United States; that file was produced by joining the individual State roads layers from the National Highway Planning Network (NHPN), Geographic Data Technology (GDT) Dynamap, and TIGER/Line files. This layer is part of the 1997-2014 edition of the National Atlas of the United States.

Purpose:

Global Map data are part of a cooperative effort among national mapping organizations around the world to produce and maintain a standard set of basic cartographic data that supports global investigations of natural and human-built environments. Participating nations prepare data using a common data structure and description to ensure compatibility and to tangibly promote the tenets of the Global Spatial Data Infrastructure. These data are intended for geographic display and analysis at the national level, and for large regional areas. The data should be displayed and analyzed at scales appropriate for 1:1,000,000-scale data. No responsibility is assumed by the National Atlas of the United States in the use of these data.

Completeness Omission:

Evaluation Method Description:

Lineage:

Process Step:

Roads consisting of Interstates, U.S. routes, and State routes were collected from NHPN and GDT Dynamap. In areas with a sparse road network, some roads deemed major were collected from NHPN. Roads for Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands were collected from 2006 2nd Edition TIGER/Line files.

Date And Time: 2008-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The data were converted to an Albers Equal Area projection. Attribute fields for miles and kilometers were added, and the values were calculated using ArcGIS. The data were then converted back to geographic coordinates. Adjustments were made to the roads attribution to clarify descriptive information and to correct errors.

Date And Time: 2012-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

All the 1:1,000,000-scale map layers were checked for vertical integration. Line positions were adjusted as necessary to remove overlaps. Wherever possible, linear features were separated by 300 meters, but were kept within 500 meters of their true location.

Date And Time: 2012-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Roads along the Mexican border were aligned based on reference to ESRI Basemaps, Google Maps, and Bing Maps.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The map layer was saved in shapefile and in file geodatabase formats.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Data along the Canadian border were adjusted so that the Canadian and USA 1:1,000,000-scale data sets can be used together as seamlessly as possible. Problem areas were identified by Natural Resources Canada (NRCan) and edits to the USA data were performed by the USGS. To identify problems, the National Atlas 1M roads 2012 data set was projected to the NRCan working coordinate system, which is a Lambert Conformal Conic projection with standard parallels of 49 and 77 degrees, datum of NAD83, an XY tolerance of 0.001 meters, and an XY resolution of 0.0001 meters. The Canadian and US data sets were displayed together and features were compared across the border. An initial set of comments and suggestions for edits were sent to the USGS by NRCan. NRCan used satellite imagery for reference during the editing process by converting the appropriate National Atlas 1:1,000,000-scale map layers and the 250K Canadian topo data to KML and loading the KMLs into GoogleEarth. Using the cursor coordinates in the ArcMap status bar, NRCan visually navigated and correlated the ArcMap and GoogleEarth vectors. NRCan exported their findings and sent them to the USGS for correction and (or) further comment. USGS reviewed the Canadian findings and returned the data to NRCan with comments. The U.S. data were either edited, not edited and further explanation was requested, or not edited and changes were suggested to for the Canadian data. This process was repeated until both NRCan and USGS felt that the data were satisfactorily harmonized.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Roads in the Virgin Islands that were previously identified as State routes were reattributed as VI routes. Because there is a separate Ferries data set available from the National Atlas, ferries were deleted from this data set.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

Where road segments less than 0.1 KM in length were adjacent to longer segments with the same attributes, the segments were joined. All remaining roads less than 0.1 KM in length were evaluated and were either joined to neighboring segments, or their length was adjusted to greater than 0.1 KM. The data were converted to an Albers Equal Area projection, values for miles and kilometers were calculated using ArcGIS, and the data were converted back to geographic coordinates. There were several roads that needed a directional prefix added; the prefixes were obtained from the TIGER and NTAD sources.

Date And Time: 2013-01-01T00:00:00

Process Step:

The National Atlas 1M Roads data set file name was changed to reflect the Global Map file-naming convention. Existing attributes were deleted except those required by Global Map, and the attributes f_code, acc, exs, rst, med, rtt, rsu, loc, and soc were added and populated with the appropriate values. The data were checked using the Global Map Data Checker.

Date And Time: 2013-06-01T00:00:00
