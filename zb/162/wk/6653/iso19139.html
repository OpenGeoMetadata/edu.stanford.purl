Abstract:

This layer is a georeferenced raster image of a continental map of Africa titled 'Afrique Physique,' originally created by J. Migeon in 1891. The map shows the heights of peaks and depths of lakes in Africa and contains other illustrated features such as coastal islands and rivers. This map has an inset engraving of diamond fields by Fillatreau and Soudain. This map was georeferenced using a Sinusoidal projection. All map collar and inset information is also available as part of the raster image, including any inset maps, profiles, statistical tables, directories, text, illustrations, or other information associated with the principal map. This map is part of a selection of digitally scanned and georeferenced historic maps of Africa from the Oscar I. Norwich Collection at Stanford University.

Purpose:

This historic paper map provides an historical perspective of the cultural and physical landscape during this time period. The wide range of information provided on these maps make them useful in the study of historic geography. As this map has been georeferenced, it also can be used as a background layer in conjunction with other GIS data.

Statement:

This map was scanned and georeferenced by the Stanford University Geospatial Center using a Sinusoidal projection. The prime meridian used during the georeferencing process was the Paris prime meridian and the central meridian was set at 22.33723, the latitude of origin used for all regional maps was 0°. In the process of georeferencing this map, 4 control points were created. The transformation order used to georeference the map is 1st order.
