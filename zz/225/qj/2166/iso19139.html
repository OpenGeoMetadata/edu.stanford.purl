Abstract:

This layer is a georeferenced raster image displaying color-sliced elevation data for the Conterminous United States at 100-meter resolution, using an Albers Equal-Area Conic projection. Each color tint represents a range of elevation values. There are 32 elevation classes and colors required to map all 50 of the United States, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands, plus an additional class and color to represent non-U.S. land. The conterminous United States map layer contains elevation classes 1 to 31, as well as class 33 (non-U.S. land). The color-sliced elevation data were derived from National Elevation Dataset (NED) data and show the terrain of the conterminous United States at a resolution of 100 meters. The NED is a raster product assembled by the U.S. Geological Survey, designed to provide national elevation data in a seamless form with a consistent datum, elevation unit, and projection. Data corrections made in the NED assembly process minimize artifacts, permit edge matching, and fill sliver areas of missing data. This layer is part of the 1997-2014 edition of the National Atlas of the United States.

Purpose:

The 100-meter resolution color-sliced elevation data were developed to portray the terrain of the United States at 1:1,000,000 scale. They are intended primarily for visual purposes. The original NED data should be used for conducting analysis and determining the most accurate elevation values. No responsibility is assumed by the National Atlas of the United States in the use of these data.

Process Step:

The color-sliced Elevation image was derived from NatAtlas US Elevation 2012 by the following steps: The elevation data were input to the ArcGIS Reclass by ASCII File command and sliced into 32 steps as specified in the text reclass table provided with this data set. See the Supplemental Information in this metadata for a table describing the elevation limits for each step. The 32 classes were assigned RGB color values according to the color ramp (.clr) file provided with this data set. Colors were based on the Brown to Blue Green Diverging, Bright color ramp in ArcGIS. Steps (classes) 1 and 2 are reserved for below-sea-level values and ocean (elevation of 0), respectively. The 32-class color-sliced image was loaded into Photoshop. Using the land/water mask, non-U.S. land areas were set to a value of 33. In order to make elevation tints that would be directly comparable across datasets, the same remapping scheme was applied to data for the conterminous U.S., Alaska, Hawaii, and Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands. As a result, not all of the 32 elevation steps are represented in every data set. Alaska has all 32 steps represented, but the conterminous U.S. is missing step 32 (elevations higher than 5,200 meters), Hawaii is missing steps 31 and 32 (elevations higher than 4,200 meters), and the Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands data set, with a maximum elevation of 1,322 meters, has no data represented in steps 19 through 32. Ocean areas (step 2) were set to an RGB value of (193,224,250). Non-U.S. areas (step 33) were set to an RGB value of (240,235,211).
